I am an architect and educator based in Las Vegas — currently at Klai Juba Wald Architects + Interiors, contributing to landmark hospitality projects including the Guitar Tower at Hard Rock Las Vegas, LVCC Phase III, Hard Rock Athens, and Hard Rock San Juan.

My practice bridges two disciplines: precision delivery of construction documents on billion-dollar projects, and the intellectual rigor of research-led design. I hold dual M.Arch degrees in Hospitality & Entertainment Design (UNLV) and Digital Architecture Technology (Pars University, Tehran).

As Instructor at UNLV's College of Architecture, Planning, and Public Affairs, I teach architectural design studios while actively pursuing ARE licensure.

Video 01 · Group Project

Kinetic Sun-Shader — A Façade That Reads the Light

A collaborative experiment in responsive architecture: a kinetic shading screen that measures the sun's intensity and reshapes itself in real time to temper the light it lets through. The geometry was driven by a Grasshopper definition wired to an Arduino sensing rig, and the working prototype was built entirely from cardboard — a deliberately low-fi study of a high-performance idea.
